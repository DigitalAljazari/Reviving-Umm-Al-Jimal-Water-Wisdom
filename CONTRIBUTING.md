# Contributing to Reviving Umm Al-Jimal's Water Wisdom

Thank you for your interest in contributing to **Reviving Umm Al-Jimal's Water Wisdom**.

This interdisciplinary initiative connects water heritage, hydrology, archaeology, artificial intelligence, digital simulation, ecosystem restoration, education, community participation, and peacebuilding.

We welcome responsible contributions from researchers, engineers, archaeologists, ecologists, educators, students, local knowledge holders, community members, civil-society organizations, public institutions, technology specialists, and other qualified contributors.

## 2. Contribution Principles

All contributions should follow these principles:

- **Accuracy:** Claims should be supported by evidence or clearly identified as proposals.
- **Transparency:** Assumptions, limitations, uncertainties, and sources should be stated.
- **Safety:** No contribution should encourage unsafe or unverified construction.
- **Heritage respect:** Archaeological sites and historic structures must be treated responsibly.
- **Community dignity:** Local people should be represented respectfully and accurately.
- **Environmental responsibility:** Proposed interventions must consider ecological consequences.
- **Attribution:** Sources and contributors must receive appropriate acknowledgment.
- **No misrepresentation:** Proposed, simulated, tested, and completed activities must remain clearly distinguished.
- **Non-exclusivity:** Contribution does not create exclusive control over the project's identity or partnerships.
- **Public value:** Contributions should support research, education, resilience, and community benefit.

## 4. How to Contribute

### Open an Issue

Use a GitHub Issue to:

- Suggest a research source.
- Identify an error.
- Propose a technical improvement.
- Ask a scientific or methodological question.
- Recommend a partnership area.
- Suggest a document, diagram, dataset, or tool.
- Report a broken link or formatting problem.

An Issue should include:

- A clear title.
- A description of the proposal or problem.
- Its relevance to the project.
- Supporting evidence or references.
- Known risks or limitations.
- A proposed next step.
- The contributor's name and optional affiliation.

Do not publish private, sensitive, or confidential information in a public Issue.

### Submit a Pull Request

A Pull Request may propose:

- Corrections to existing documents.
- New research references.
- Improved explanations.
- Translation or accessibility improvements.
- New documentation.
- Code, modelling, or visualization contributions.
- Approved images or diagrams.

A Pull Request should:

- Address one clear topic.
- Explain what was changed and why.
- Identify sources and evidence.
- Disclose limitations or conflicts of interest.
- Avoid unrelated modifications.
- Respect the repository's licensing requirements.

Submission does not guarantee acceptance.

### Contact the Project Directly

Contact the project directly when a contribution involves:

- Formal research partnerships.
- Engineering or technical services.
- Funding or equipment.
- Confidential information.
- Sensitive maps or land information.
- Identifiable community participants.
- Unpublished research.
- Intellectual property.
- Commercial use.
- Institutional or governmental agreements.

**Email:** contact@digitalaljazari.org

## 5. Research and Citation Standards

Research contributions should:

- Prefer peer-reviewed research, official data, technical standards, and authoritative heritage sources.
- Provide complete and verifiable references.
- Distinguish primary evidence from secondary interpretation.
- Indicate the geographical and climatic context.
- Identify uncertainty, conflicting evidence, and data gaps.
- Avoid fabricated or unverifiable citations.
- Avoid assuming that a solution effective elsewhere will work at Deir Al-Kahf.
- Respect copyright and quotation limitations.

A recommended reference should include:

- Author or organization.
- Title.
- Year.
- Publication or publisher.
- DOI or stable link where available.
- A brief explanation of its relevance.

## 6. Technical and Water-Safety Requirements

Technical proposals must state whether they are:

- Preliminary ideas.
- Conceptual designs.
- Simulated scenarios.
- Expert recommendations.
- Technically reviewed designs.
- Field-tested interventions.
- Verified results.

Technical contributions should address:

- Site-specific conditions.
- Rainfall and runoff assumptions.
- Soil and infiltration.
- Storage calculations.
- Sediment management.
- Overflow and drainage.
- Evaporation.
- Water quality.
- Human and animal safety.
- Inspection and maintenance.
- Environmental effects.
- Applicable approvals.

The project's current conceptual sequence is:

**Runoff channels → protected sedimentation basin → covered main cistern or tank → drip irrigation network → recharge and infiltration garden**

Proposals must respect these safety principles:

- No large, deep, unprotected open-water structures.
- Preference for covered primary storage.
- Limited exposed water surfaces.
- Safe inspection and maintenance access.
- Protection of children, visitors, livestock, and wildlife.
- Controlled sediment and overflow management.
- Efficient water distribution.
- Appropriate water-quality safeguards.

Repository materials do not constitute construction authorization. Physical implementation requires qualified professional review, safeguards, consultation, and legal approvals.

## 7. Ecological Contributions

Ecological proposals should:

- Prioritize locally appropriate native species.
- Avoid invasive or water-intensive plants.
- Consider soil, rainfall, grazing, salinity, and maintenance.
- Explain the evidence supporting species recommendations.
- Address long-term water demand and plant survival.
- Consider biodiversity and habitat effects.
- Avoid unsupported restoration claims.

Candidate species must not be planted solely because they are mentioned in project documents. Final selection requires site-specific ecological assessment.

---

## 8. Community and Local Knowledge

Contributions involving community knowledge should:

- Respect knowledge holders.
- Obtain appropriate consent.
- Provide fair acknowledgment.
- Preserve cultural and environmental context.
- Avoid unrelated commercial exploitation.
- Consider benefit-sharing where appropriate.
- Protect confidential or sensitive knowledge.
- Avoid speaking for communities without authorization.

Women, young people, farmers, pastoral communities, educators, and local residents should be treated as knowledge partners rather than only as beneficiaries.

## 9. Images and Field Documentation

Before contributing an image, confirm that:

- You created it or have permission to submit it.
- Appropriate consent was obtained from identifiable individuals.
- Its caption and context are accurate.
- It does not expose sensitive information.
- It has not been misleadingly altered.
- Ownership and reuse conditions are clear.

Do not submit:

- Images copied without permission.
- AI-generated images presented as field evidence.
- Images exposing private documents or sensitive locations.
- Photographs that undermine participant dignity or safety.

---

## 10. Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence

AI may support research organization, modelling, translation, visualization, drafting, and education.

Contributors using AI should:

- Verify facts, calculations, and citations.
- Disclose material AI assistance where relevant.
- Never present AI output as verified field evidence.
- Avoid fabricated references.
- Protect confidential information.
- Document important assumptions.
- Keep human experts responsible for technical decisions.
- Distinguish simulation from observation.
- Respect copyright and data rights.

AI does not replace archaeological, hydrological, ecological, engineering, legal, or community expertise.

---

## 11. Rights, Attribution, and Recognition

Contributors must confirm that:

- They created the submitted material or have authority to share it.
- It does not knowingly violate copyright, privacy, confidentiality, or other rights.
- Sources and third-party materials are identified.
- Required consent has been obtained.
- The project may review, edit, publish, preserve, and display an accepted contribution with appropriate attribution.

Accepted contributors may be recognized through:

- GitHub contribution history.
- Document acknowledgments.
- Named authorship where appropriate.
- Technical-review recognition.
- Academic authorship according to recognized standards.

Submitting a contribution does not create employment, ownership, exclusive partnership, institutional representation, or authority to speak for the project.

Major datasets, software, commissioned work, technical designs, confidential materials, or jointly owned intellectual property may require separate written agreements.

See [LICENSE.md](LICENSE.md) for repository use and licensing terms.

## 12. Review and Conduct

Contributions may be reviewed for:

- Relevance.
- Accuracy.
- Evidence quality.
- Safety.
- Environmental responsibility.
- Heritage protection.
- Community dignity.
- Legal and ethical risk.
- Clarity and accessibility.
- Compatibility with the project roadmap.
- Licensing and permission requirements.

The project may accept, request revisions, seek expert review, postpone, or decline a contribution.

Contributors must communicate respectfully. The following are not acceptable:

- Harassment or discrimination.
- Personal attacks.
- Deliberate misinformation.
- Plagiarism.
- False partnership claims.
- Exploitation of community knowledge.
- Unsafe technical instructions.
- Unauthorized commercial promotion.
- Publication of private information.
- Appropriation of the project's identity or another contributor's work.
