# Media Coverage and Field Visit Documentation

This page compiles selected media coverage and public documentation of the developing *Reviving Umm Al-Jimal's Water Wisdom* project.

The coverage is organized around two fieldwork phases. The first phase was exploratory and documentary. The second phase was practical and preparatory, connecting the water heritage of Umm Al-Jimal with the proposed Deir Al-Kahf pilot landscape and the recovery of native vegetation.

Media links are provided as a public record of coverage. Responsibility for the wording and content of each published article remains with its publisher.

---

## First Field Visit: Exploration and Documentation

**Period:** June 2026  
**Focus:** Umm Al-Jimal and comparable basaltic and desert landscapes in Jordan's Northern Badia.

This phase focused on observing and documenting the historic water-harvesting system, basalt architecture, cisterns, channels, runoff pathways, and the wider environmental context. It also explored how the underlying water-management logic could inform digital simulation, education, research, and future dryland applications.

## Second Field Visit: Practical Site Assessment and Preparatory Work

**Period:** July 2026  
**Locations:** Umm Al-Jimal and Deir Al-Kahf, Northern Badia, Jordan.

This phase moved the project from initial exploration toward practical preparation. The visit connected lessons from Umm Al-Jimal's historic water system with the Deir Al-Kahf landscape, considered water harvesting and native vegetation recovery together, identified a proposed pilot location, and initiated preliminary site assessment and groundwork for the next design stage.

The proposed pilot remains subject to professional topographic, hydrological, soil, ecological, engineering, safety, legal, and community review before physical implementation.
