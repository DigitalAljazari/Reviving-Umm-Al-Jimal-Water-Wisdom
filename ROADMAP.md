# Project Roadmap

## Reviving Umm Al-Jimal's Water Wisdom

This roadmap presents the phased development of **Reviving Umm Al-Jimal's Water Wisdom**, from heritage research and field documentation to digital simulation, technical validation, pilot implementation, monitoring, and responsible replication.

The roadmap is designed to remain flexible. Activities may be adjusted according to scientific findings, community priorities, technical requirements, environmental safeguards, available resources, and legal approvals.

---

## Status Key

- ✅ Completed or substantially completed
- 🔄 In progress
- ⏳ Planned
- 🔍 Requires technical validation
- 🤝 Requires partnership or funding
- 📌 Long-term development

## Phase 1: Concept Development and Heritage Research

Status: ✅ Completed and continuing

### Main Activities

- Define the central project concept.
- Study the historic water wisdom of Umm Al-Jimal.
- Examine the relationship between basalt architecture, rainfall, channels, reservoirs, cisterns, sedimentation, overflow, and surrounding land.
- Identify the contemporary relevance of historic water-management principles.
- Connect water heritage with climate adaptation, ecosystem recovery, education, and peacebuilding.
- Develop the initial project narrative and public identity.
- Begin reviewing relevant archaeological, environmental, hydrological, and technological knowledge.

### Main Outputs

- Clear interdisciplinary project concept.
- Initial interpretation of Umm Al-Jimal's water-system logic.
- Public project descriptions and educational materials.
- Preliminary research questions.
- Initial digital and visual explanations.

---

## Phase 2: Field Documentation

Status: ✅ Initial activities completed; 🔄 documentation continuing

### Main Activities

- Conduct preliminary field observation at Umm Al-Jimal.
- Document visible water-system relationships.
- Photograph relevant heritage and landscape features.
- Visit the proposed Deir Al-Kahf pilot landscape.
- Document basalt terrain, exposed soil, vegetation, surrounding agriculture, and possible runoff patterns.
- Undertake early spatial measurement and site observation.
- Record preliminary opportunities, risks, and knowledge gaps.
- Organize field images and documentation for research and public communication.

### Main Outputs

- Initial photographic field record.
- Preliminary site observations.
- Fieldwork documentation file.
- Identification of required technical studies.
- Initial comparison between the historic source landscape and the proposed pilot landscape.

For additional information, see [Fieldwork and Site Documentation](FIELDWORK.md).

## Phase 3: Baseline Studies and Technical Assessment

Status: ⏳ Planned; 🔍 technical validation required; 🤝 partnerships and funding required

### Required Studies

- Professional topographic survey.
- Catchment delineation.
- Rainfall and climate-data analysis.
- Hydrological and runoff modelling.
- Soil-depth and soil-texture assessment.
- Infiltration and permeability testing.
- Salinity and erosion assessment.
- Geotechnical investigation where required.
- Ecological baseline study.
- Native vegetation assessment.
- Water-demand estimation.
- Community and stakeholder consultation.
- Environmental and social risk screening.
- Legal, land-use, and approval review.

### Decision Gate

The project will proceed to detailed physical design only if the baseline studies confirm that the proposed intervention is technically feasible, environmentally responsible, socially beneficial, and safe.

### Expected Outputs

- Verified baseline report.
- Topographic and hydrological maps.
- Soil and ecological assessment.
- Community-consultation findings.
- Preliminary environmental and social safeguards.
- Confirmed or revised pilot location and design assumptions.

## Phase 4: Digital Reconstruction and AI-Assisted Simulation

Status: 🔄 Concept development in progress; ⏳ full development planned

### Main Activities

- Digitally map the operating logic of Umm Al-Jimal's historic water system.
- Develop a terrain model for the proposed pilot landscape.
- Integrate rainfall, runoff, soil, storage, irrigation, and vegetation data.
- Simulate alternative water-harvesting configurations.
- Compare storage capacity and evaporation-reduction options.
- Examine sedimentation and overflow scenarios.
- Model irrigation demand under different planting scenarios.
- Explore the effects of climate variability.
- Develop accessible educational visualizations.
- Subject AI-generated findings to scientific and technical review.

### Expected Outputs

- Digital representation of the historic water-system logic.
- AI-assisted scenario-comparison tools.
- Rainfall-runoff and storage simulations.
- Evaporation and irrigation scenarios.
- Educational digital models.
- Documented assumptions, limitations, and validation requirements.

## Phase 5: Detailed Pilot Design

Status: ⏳ Planned; dependent on Phase 3 and Phase 4

### Proposed Design Sequence

**Runoff channels → protected sedimentation basin → covered main cistern or tank → drip irrigation network → recharge and infiltration garden**

### Main Activities

- Define verified runoff-collection areas.
- Design erosion-control and water-guidance measures.
- Size the protected sedimentation basin.
- Determine safe sediment-cleaning procedures.
- Select and size the covered main storage system.
- Design inspection, maintenance, and safety access.
- Develop controlled overflow routes.
- Design the drip-irrigation network.
- Select appropriate native vegetation.
- Develop the infiltration and ecological-learning area.
- Evaluate shade or solar-panel options where appropriate.
- Prepare construction drawings, specifications, quantities, and cost estimates.
- Conduct independent technical and safety review.

### Design Principles

- No large, deep, uncovered ponds.
- Covered primary storage.
- Reduced exposed water surfaces.
- Protection of people, animals, and wildlife.
- Efficient water use.
- Controlled sediment and overflow.
- Ecologically appropriate planting.
- Practical inspection and maintenance.
- Compliance with applicable requirements and approvals.

### Expected Outputs

- Complete technical design package.
- Environmental and social management measures.
- Safety and maintenance plan.
- Bill of quantities and detailed budget.
- Implementation schedule.
- Monitoring framework.

## Phase 10: Responsible Replication

Status: 📌 Long-term development

Replication will begin only after the pilot has produced sufficient verified evidence.

### Replication Principles

- Adapt rather than copy.
- Conduct site-specific studies.
- Respect local climate, terrain, soil, ecology, and culture.
- Use verified data.
- Consult local communities.
- Protect people and animals.
- Avoid large exposed water surfaces.
- Maintain transparent costs and performance claims.
- Document both successful and unsuccessful results.
- Preserve the public value of shared learning.

### Potential Replication Contexts

- Other locations in Jordan's Badia.
- Arid and semi-arid communities in the Middle East.
- Water-stressed heritage landscapes.
- Dryland schools, farms, and community-learning sites.
- Comparable basalt and desert environments worldwide.

### Expected Outputs

- Replication assessment framework.
- Site-selection guidance.
- Adaptable technical and educational tools.
- Policy recommendations.
- Regional and international knowledge exchange.

## Immediate Priorities

The project's immediate priorities are:

1. Complete the preliminary evidence and document archive.
2. Build a multidisciplinary technical advisory network.
3. Secure resources for baseline studies.
4. Conduct professional topographic, hydrological, soil, and ecological assessments.
5. Develop the first verified digital terrain and water-flow model.
6. Consult local stakeholders and knowledge holders.
7. Prepare environmental, social, safety, and maintenance safeguards.
8. Refine the pilot design based on verified evidence.
9. Develop an initial monitoring and evaluation framework.
10. Continue responsible public communication and partnership development.
